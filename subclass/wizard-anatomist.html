The Anatomist

A wizard who commits to the study of life, and the bodies of organisms, for the sake of sculpting the flesh like an artist might manipulate any other medium.

2nd-Level - Study of Medicine

You gain proficiency in the medicine skill at 2nd level, if you didn‚Äôt have it already. If you are already proficient in medicine, you gain expertise instead. You may choose to use intelligence or wisdom as your base ability when making a medicine check.

2nd-Level - Fleshcrafting

Additionally at 2nd level, you gain the ability to alter the properties of your body to adapt to various situations, you gain one of the following abilities, which you may exchange for another ability on this list over the course of a long rest:

  • Air Sacs: You gain the ability to hold your breath for up to an hour without suffering any adverse effects. When this hour ends, you begin suffocating as normal.
  • Clawed: Your hands end in sharp claws, allowing you to make claw attacks. These attacks deal 1d6 slashing plus your strength modifier. When you attack with a claw, or a melee weapon, if you aren‚Äôt wielding anything in your off hand, you may use a bonus action to make a claw attack, adding your strength modifier to the damage of the attack.
  • Devouring: You may consume any organic matter, no matter how wretched or disgusting it might be, and not suffer any adverse effects. You have advantage on saving throws against poison and disease, and immunity to any disease you might suffer from ingesting something. Additionally, by consuming at least one pound of organic matter in this way as an action, you heal hit points equal to 1d4 + your Intelligence modifier. You may do this a number of times equal to your proficiency modifier, regaining all uses on a long rest.
  • Excavator: You gain a burrow speed equal to half your walking speed.
  • Vitruvian: You have a secondary set of arms in addition to your primary two. These arms function as your normal arms do, with two exceptions:
    1. You cannot engage in multi-weapon fighting with your additional arms
    2. You cannot hold a shield in your secondary arms
    While you have these arms, you gain an additional free object interaction on your turn.

6th-Level - Master Fleshcrafter

At 6th level, you may grant any of the abilities from your Fleshcrafting to any willing creature over the course of a long rest. A creature may still only have one ability from this feature at a time.

Transfusion

Additionally at 6th level, when you score a critical hit on a creature, or reduce a creature to 0hp, you can use a bonus action to pull the blood from its body to heal yourself or another willing creature a number of hit points equal to your level, plus your intelligence modifier. This ability does not work against any creature that wouldn‚Äôt have blood (most likely elements, constructs, and many undead).

10th-Level Homunculus

At 10th level, you create a creature in your own image. Your homunculus creature uses a humanoid stat block with a CR equal to 1/3rd of your own or less, and any one of the abilities available to you through your fleshcrafting feature (you choose this feature when you create the homunculus). You determine how the homunculus appears when you create it.

You control your homunculus in combat, and it takes its actions on your turn. If the homunculus is ever killed or destroyed, you may create a new one over the course of a day, gaining 2 levels of exhaustion in the process as you use your own vitality to bring the creature to life.

You may only have one homunculus at a time.

14th-Level - Mind over Matter

Starting at 14th level, you are so familiar with the nature of your own body that you may use your knowledge of it to strengthen it against all harm. When you make a saving throw you may use a reaction to expend a spell slot, adding half the slot‚Äôs level (rounded up) to the total of your roll.

Additionally, when you fail a saving throw to maintain concentration on a spell, you can choose to succeed instead. You may use this ability in this way once, regaining the use of it after completing a short or long rest.

20th Level - Self Beyond Self

Starting at 20th level, you may create a number of homunculi equal to your intelligence modifier. You may communicate telepathically with each of your homunculi across any distance, even across realities or planes of existence. Additionally, whenever you have at least one homunculi active, you gain the following benefits, as the weaknesses of your body are carried by your creation:

  • You are immune to nonmagical bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage.
  • Magic can no longer effect your mind. You are immune to the charmed and frightened conditions, and if a spell would in any way force you to do something you wouldn‚Äôt do normally, it fails.
  • As a bonus action, you may enter a state of intense concentration (as if concentrating on a spell), when you cast any spell of 5th level or below while in this state, you do not expend a spell slot. You may remain in this state for a minute, or until your concentration ends. You must take a long rest before you can use this ability again.

If you‚Äôre ever killed, you may choose to instantly transfer your consciousness to one of your homunculi, living on in the body of the homunculi. You retain all class levels, as well as your intelligence, wisdom, and charisma scores when you do this, while the homunculi‚Äôs strength, dexterity, and constitution scores replace your own. When you overtake a homunculi in this way, the homunculi themselves is instantly killed in mind as their consciousness is replaced by your own.
