Violence Domain

There is a common misunderstanding of what a Hierophant is, and what exactly it is that they do. Many see them as a guiding light, a teacher and savior of the people bringing the gifts of gods to the mortals. But this is not entirely true. A Hierophant merely represents one aspect of a god's being, and the mortal connection to it. As many Hierophants who are saviors, just as many represent the more primal or wrathful sides of the divine.

As a Herald of Violence, you have come to embrace one such aspect of your god. Violence is not just a means of harm, but a connection to the very roots of mortal being, closely intertwined with the heavenly nature of existence. Such is the way of things. And why should we reject who we really are?

1st-Level - Violence Adept

As a Herald of Violence, the ability to cause harm comes to you naturally. At 1st level, you gain proficiency with Martial weapons, and may cast damaging spells without a focus or material components.

1st-Level - Violence of the Mind

Additionally at 1st level, the ability to cause harm comes to you naturally. You gain proficiency with Martial weapons, and may cast damaging spells without a focus or material components.

3rd-Level - No Pain No Gain

At 3rd level, you empower those around you in battle, allowing your allies to better partake in divine bloodbath at the cost of your personal wellbeing. You gain a new Give of Yourself option. When you sacrifice your hit die, you lose AC equivalent to the number of hit dice you sacrificed. Afterwards, all allies within 60 feet of you each roll the hit dice you sacrificed and gain that amount in temporary hit points. Additionally, as a bonus action on their turn, an affected creature may make a weapon attack. The creature can do this a number of times equal to the hit die you expend. These additional attacks are lost after one minute.

7th-Level - Bestial Frenzy

At 7th level, you emanate an aura which can cause your allies to enter an animalistic bloodthirst, allowing them to ignore injuries and cause grievous wounds. As an action, you may unleash this aura. For the next minute, all creatures which you designate within 60 feet of you immediately heal an amount equal to your Wisdom modifier upon taking damage. Additionally, when a designated creature reduces another creature to 0 hit points, they gain temporary hit points equivalent to half the damage dealt on the killing blow.

Also while this aura is active, when a designated creature critically strikes another with a weapon attack, you may spend your reaction to exacerbate the injury. The attacked creature must make a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 + your Proficiency bonus + your Wisdom score). Upon a failure, they begin Bleeding if they were attacked with a slashing or piercing weapon, or gain a Fracture if they were attacked with a bludgeoning weapon.

11th-Level - Rivers of Blood

Your aura of bloodlust grows even stronger. You need not spend your reaction to grant an additional injury when a designated creature in your Bestial Frenzy makes a critical strike.

14th-Level feature

Inside even the most stoic and rational individuals lies a dormant animal, one which you‚Äôve learned to awaken through a sacred ritual at 14th level. This ritual lasts one minute, and requires an area at least 15 feet square in space, 50gp of common casting materials, and the sacrifice of a living animal weighing at least 75 lbs or larger.

Upon the start of this ritual, all creatures in a 100 foot radius are made aware of your presence and immediately become hostile. During this minute, you are Vulnerable, and must devote any and all actions to the completion of the ritual. If at any point during this process you take damage, roll as you would for keeping concentration on a spell. If you fail, the ritual is lost. If you devote any action (including free actions, bonus actions, and reactions) to anything other than the ritual, or if you are prevented or restricted from speaking or moving (as with the Silence spell or the Restrained condition, respectively), the ritual is also lost.

Upon successful completion of this ritual, you may designate any number of creatures you know to be immune. Every other creature within a 100 foot radius enters a crazed madness and possesses an unquenchable thirst for violence for the next 5 minutes. If they see another creature, they must spend any and all actions available attacking it by any means, or spend all movement options moving towards that creature if they cannot attack it from where they are. If they cannot see another creature, they must spend all actions available to them searching for one. If they choose not to comply on their turn, they immediately take 5d6 Psychic damage. This effect ends on any affected creatures only after the 5 minutes is up.
