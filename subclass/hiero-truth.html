Truth Domain

Learning the truth is a curious thing, as lies are easy to swallow, but very often the bare truth is more difficult, raising questions and answering few. Truth is an object of value, in a world where lying is often so easy. To be “of” truth, one must be a sort of pillar themselves, of reality, of objectivity, of honesty. To bear the weight that is Truth as an aspect, one might lead a life that is not entirely their own, seeing the dishonesty in others, and to be met with the disconcerting revelation that very often, the truth only raises more questions than it answers. A revelation that buries itself in the very core of this aspect, as the further into the truth one goes, the broader these truths become. Lies told by a friend to hide some secret become vast schools of understanding, rendered totally false in the face of the bare realities of the world. Terrible realities that man is not truly equipped to understand.

But one must understand, they must understand when this path is laid out before them, that their truths will always only raise more questions than they answer.

1st-Level - Discern and Compel

At 1st level, you may use an action to impose your power on any creature you can see, forcing them to make a wisdom saving throw, on a failed save, this creature is unable to speak a lie for one minute. You must finish a short or long rest before using this ability again.

Additionally, you gain proficiency in the insight skill. If you already have insight proficiency, you instead gain expertise.

3rd-Level - Faith Unblinded

At 3rd level, you gain a new way to use your Give of Yourself ability. As a bonus action, you may choose to blind yourself temporarily, willingly taking the blinded condition and gaining visions into multiple possible futures, straining your mind and body to peer into the myriad truths of all possible outcomes.

While blinded in this way, you may use your reaction to expend hit die after a d20 roll is made, adding or subtracting the total of your roll (or rolls) from the total of the original d20 roll as you use your visions to guide an ally through a trial, or warn them of an oncoming danger.

7th-Level - Speaker of Names

At 7th level, you are able to discern truths about a creature more personal than those that lie on the surface. As an action, you may observe a creature, making an insight check contested by a deception check from your target creature. On a success, you learn the creature’s true name. Following this, you may use a bonus action to speak this creature’s name. Based on the way, and the context in which you speak a creature’s name, you may grant different effects. These effects are as follows:

  • Grant the creature a number of temporary hit points equal to your level, plus your wisdom modifier.

  • Remove any of the following conditions from a creature: charmed, frightened, insane, stunned, petrified, poisoned, and unconscious. You may only wake an unconscious creature if they have more than 0 hit points.

  • Issue a command, as per the command spell.

  • Cause a panic in a creature, forcing them to reckon with their sense of self. When you name a creature in this way they must make a wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, the creature takes 8d6 psychic damage, and is incapacitated for one minute. At the end of each of the creature’s turns, they take 1d6 psychic damage, and may attempt the saving throw again.

11th-Level - Beacon of Truth

At 11th level, you become a guiding light for your allies, allowing them to perceive the true nature of things more easily. As an action, you grant any creatures you designate with enhanced wisdom, providing them with a bonus to all Investigation, Insight, and Perception checks equivalent to your Wisdom modifier. Additionally, these creatures have the ability to see any invisible creatures or objects. These benefits last for one minute, and you may use this ability a number of times equivalent to your proficiency bonus, regaining all uses upon a long rest.

14th-Level - The Silhouette of a Whale Passes Beneath A Small Fishing Vessel

At 14th level, you gain the ability to invoke some much greater power. The incomprehensible that lies both beyond and within reality. You call upon it, allowing it to pass through the area around you, a reckoning of truth in its purest form for all present with you. A truth others are not equipped to understand.

You become Vulnerable, spending your action for your next three turns as if you were casting a spell. Afterwards, all creatures you choose within a 100 foot radius of you must make an Intelligence saving throw. On a successful save, they take 15d6 psychic damage. On a failed save, they take the damage, and gain the insane condition. For each creature who fails the save, roll a d4, on this d4 a 1 represents hours, a 2 represents days, a 3 represents weeks, and a 4 represents months. After rolling this d4, roll 1d100, the result of this d100 roll is the length of time the creature is insane (as per the condition), using the unit of time rolled on the d4.

If a creature critically fails this save (rolling a natural 1) they do not gain the insane condition, but instead gain both the prone, and stunned conditions, and are unable to speak coherently, as they fall to the ground babbling madly. When rolling a d4 to determine the length of time these creatures will be effected, a 1 represents days, a 2 represents weeks, a 3 represents months, and a 4 represents years.

You may use this ability once, regaining the use of it upon a long rest. If at any point during this process you take damage, roll as you would for keeping concentration on a spell. If you fail, this ability fails and is lost until you regain its use. This ability is also lost if you cast another spell during the process, or if you are prevented or restricted from speaking or moving (as with the Silence spell or the Restrained condition, respectively).
